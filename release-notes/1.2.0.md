# UniGeek Firmware v1.2.0

**The Swiss Army Knife for ESP32 Security Research**
One firmware. Six devices. Dozens of tools — right in your pocket.

## What's New

**M5StickC Plus 2 — Now Supported**
The smallest pentesting device just got a sibling. Full support with rotary encoder navigation, PWM brightness control, and buzzer feedback. Same powerful toolkit, even more portable.

**Karma Attack**
Automatically responds to WiFi probe requests from nearby devices. When a phone searches for a known network, UniGeek answers — becoming that network instantly. See how devices blindly trust remembered SSIDs.

**MITM Attack**
Full man-in-the-middle on local networks. ARP spoofing redirects traffic through your device, letting you intercept packets in real time.

**Rogue DHCP**
Deploy a rogue DHCP server that hands out custom gateway and DNS settings. Silently redirect traffic without touching ARP tables.

**Deauth Burst**
Concentrated deauth frames at high speed. Faster and more effective than single-frame deauth.

**CCTV Sniffer**
Scan your local network for CCTV cameras and stream their feeds directly on device. Discover unsecured cameras and test their accessibility.

**Storage Checker & Device Status**
Monitor storage health, firmware version, board info, and memory usage at a glance.

## Getting Started

1. Download the `.bin` file for your device from https://github.com/lshaf/unigeek/releases
2. Flash using ESP Web Flasher or `esptool.py`
3. Power on and start exploring

**SD Card content** — For features like DuckyScript, Evil Twin portals, passwords, and QR codes, you can either:
- Copy the `sdcard/` folder contents to your SD card root, **or**
- Use the built-in **Download** menu inside the firmware to fetch them directly
